The problem with voiceover narration

Over the past days I decided that for this letter I would write about about a specific element in movies. IMO, this element is, usually, a big red flag üö©.

I'm talking about the voiceover narration.

The role of a background / off-screen narrator is not always well defined. He (or she) should give some information that are not directly staged from the screenplay.
The narrator should "walk" the spectator through the movie. The goal is to make sure that anyone who's watching the movie is not missing any important information.

Why this should be a problem?

The Office, episode 14 season 2. We see Jim (John Krasinski) listening to Pam's (Jenna Fisher) voicemail. The act of listening is actually off-screen. We here Pam's voice while jim is already leaving the office.
An incredible sequence: no narrators, no dialogues, only images edited perfectly and we get all the information we need...

The problem is when the narrator fills the lack of a proper mise-en-sc√®ne!

Movies are about images. If the author (could be the director or anyone in the production) is not able to communicate through images and he needs a background narrator in order to explain what is happening on-screen, well that is kind of a bad direction... üòï

The most obnoxious example of this kind of "bad direction" that comes to my mind is actually not from a movie, but from a tv show. Money Heist (La casa de papel, the original title).

I assume you all know the plot of this crap. It is basically a latino america soap opera with a bunch of idiots trying to rob the Royal Mint of Spain.
During the show we are constantly updated by this external narrator (I think it was a woman in the first season) about what is happening, what the characters are thinking, what they are doing, what they are going to do etc... I mean... don't tell me, show it to me!!

To conclude, my opinion is that this narrator-trick is often used by authors that are just total unqualified for the job.
