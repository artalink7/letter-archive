... The unexpected connection

On july 22nd, Ozzy Osbourne passed away. If you ever approached heavy metal music, you already know Ozzy had a topical influence on the genre.
His career was quite long. He started singing with Black Sabbath at the end of the sixties, and his last concert was just 2 weeks before his death.

Black Sabbath's first album (titled Black Sabbath and containing the title track Black Sabbath ... I mean, wtf?) did not gain immediate success and the general critics were quite negative.
This was mainly due to the gloomy atmosphere and dark lyrics in their compositions. Allusions to occultism and satanic cult are definitely not hidden in the songs. On top of that, the unusual album cover contributed to create a curious goth aesthetic.

Black Sabbath, Black Sabbath.
Apparently they pick the name from the italian movie "I tre volti della paura", distributed in the U.K. as "Black Sabbath"

After some disagreement with the band (late 70s), Ozzy left Black Sabbath and started his own solo project. However, he kept the same aesthetic and, somehow, declared himself as "The Prince of Darkness".

Who was Mr. Crowley?
Born in 1875, Aleister Crowley was one of the most famous occultist of the 20th century. He created a religion called Thelema and joined a number of esoteric covenants. He was also into alpinism and took part in some expeditions in south america and India.

Why am I telling you this?
Well, some time before Ozzy's death I was reading a collection of stories written by sicilian author Leonardo Sciascia, called Il mare colore del vino ...

The stories are set in Sicily between the 19th and the 20th century and the author combine fictitious and real facts. One of the stories is indeed about the infamous Mr. Crowley.
Through the story we come to know that the people from Cefal√π did not support the presence of Crowley's cult in the town... How could we not see that coming really??

üé¨ Weekly Movie

For this week's movie pick, I have chosen The Ninth Gate directed by Roman Polanski.

Balkan (Frank Langella) and Corso (Johnny Depp) inspecting a mysterious book.

Polanski is probably my favourite director. He was one of the first to set horror movies at present times. Of course, Rosemary's baby is his true horror-masterpiece. But, come one, picking Rosemary's baby was too easy.

The Ninth Gate is a horror-thriller movie released in 1999. The protagonist is Dean Corso (Johnny Depp), a book hunter. His job consist in acquiring old, prestigious versions of famous books and reselling them to private collectors.

The story is about an investigation concerning a particular book called The Nine Gates of the Kingdom of Shadows. It's said the book is somehow connected to the cult of Satan. Because of this, Dean will meet lots of dangerous and weird people during his quest. I'm talking about Aleister Crowley-type-of weirdos.

While watching the movie, what fascinated me the most is the real, physical attraction towards the rare books. If hard-cover books are your kink, that's your go-to movie.

There is a maniacal attention to details. Every set is extremely well designed. All the sequences in the libraries are amazing.

Although being classified as a "horror" movie, I would suggest to watch the movie also to people who usually don't like horror. Nowdays we would say it is more of a thriller than a horror. No jump scares, but a lot of tension üëçüèª
