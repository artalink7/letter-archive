Most of the sci-fi movies are set in the future. Naturally, with old sci-fi movies, it can happen that the time when the movie is set is comparable with our present-day time.
It is interesting to compare the technolgy portraited in the movies vs the one we created for real.

IMO, there are two key aspects of our life that these old sci-fi movies failed to predict: internet and the advent of the digital era.

Even the most advanced objects in these movies are chunky, full-of-buttons, analogic devices. Take spaceships as an example. In Alien, the crew members travel inside the Nostromo, a spaceship capable of interplanetary missions... Yet using some cathode ray tube screens.

Alien, screenshot from the opening scene. Released in 1979, the movie is set in a distopic future in 2122.

Actually, there is one technology we are now using in our life and that's also portraited in these movies. It is artificial intelligence.

2001: A space odissey. HAL 9000 is the red-eye and superintelligent computer guiding the crew through its mission.

For this week's movie pick, I have chosen Her directed by Spike Jonze.

Theodore (Joaquin Phoenix) and Amy (Amy Adams)

Her was released in 2013, some year before the A.I. fever. However, the movie is very current.

The protagonist is Theodore, a writer. He has to deal with a painful breakup and it seems he can't find the right support from the people around him. He finds comfort in playing video games, staying home, avoiding social contacts as much as possible.
The situation changes when Theodore installs a new intelligent OS, Samantha (Scarlett Johansson), in his computer. Samantha is charming and sweet (not quite like HAL 9000). The two of them communicate at any time through a special earphone Theodore wears all day long. They start a "real" relationship.
The relationship will evolve more and more, until Theodore starts questioning himself about what he really wants.

What does Theodore want? Probably he wants what we all want, just being happy I guess üòÖ to do so we need to bond with people ü´Ç and sometime it requires arguing, fighting, dealing with the irrational part of our personality. A.I. can't really help with that üòï

The plot is quite simple, no particular action or twists. What catches the eyes is a beautiful color palette tilted towards red, orange and pink.

Big up also for Scarlett Johansson. We only hear her voice, still it seems she really is in the scene.
